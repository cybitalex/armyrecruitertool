### 1. Register Your Copyright (Highly Recommended)

**Why**: While copyright exists automatically, registration provides additional legal benefits:
- Public record of your ownership
- Ability to sue for infringement in federal court
- Eligibility for statutory damages ($750-$150,000 per work)
- Eligibility for attorney's fees
- Prima facie evidence of validity

**How to Register**:

#### Option A: Online Registration (Recommended - $65)
1. Visit: https://www.copyright.gov/registration/
2. Click "Register a Copyright"
3. Create an account on eCO (Electronic Copyright Office)
4. Select "Literary Works" (for software)
5. Fill out Form TX or Form CO
6. Upload deposit copy (source code - can be first 25 and last 25 pages)
7. Pay $65 filing fee
8. Receive certificate in 3-8 months

#### Option B: Paper Registration ($125)
1. Download Form TX from copyright.gov
2. Print and complete the form
3. Mail with check and deposit copy to:
   ```
   Library of Congress
   Copyright Office
   101 Independence Avenue SE
   Washington, DC 20559-6000
   ```

**What to Include**:
- Title: "Army Recruiter Tool"
- Author: Alex Moran
- Year of Creation: 2025
- Year of First Publication: 2025
- Deposit: First 25 and last 25 pages of source code

**Timeline**: 3-8 months for processing
